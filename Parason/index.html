Publications in journals such as Nature or Science are extremely prestigious, because the articles published in these journals are often cited, and the author is gaining wide popularity outside his field of science. Thus, the impact factor Nature in 2009 was equal to 34.48, ie each article in the two years that have passed since its publication, on average, is cited more than 34 times. The selection criteria for articles in Nature (as well as in Science ) are extremely strict. A large number of articles are eliminated before the peer review stage, because the results of the research described in the article proposed for publication in Nature should represent a very significant advancement in a particular field of science.

According to another version, the idea of ​​creating YouTube arose when Hurley, Chen and Karim wanted to share photos and videos taken during the party, and it turned out that the Internet does not have a convenient service for posting videos [9] [13] . It was then decided to create a site where users could easily upload short videos, which were then automatically recoded into flash video format , which would allow them to view videos in most Internet browsers [14] . The first video lasting 19 seconds under the title "I am Zoo" appeared online April 23, 2005 [9] [10] [15] . It was hosted by one of the company's founders, Javed Karim . Initially, the service was not popular, but in July 2005, after the introduction of a number of innovations, including the ability to leave comments on videos and insert videos from YouTube in posts on other sites, blogs, the number of site visitors began to grow rapidly. In December 2005, the number of daily hits has amounted to 8 million, and by July 2006 their number increased to 100 million, which, according to YouTube, constituted 60% of all views online videos on the Internet [16] [10] [17] . The site used Flash Video (FLV) technology , which allows you to get good recording quality with a small amount of transmitted data. The project has become a good means of entertainment and, having formed its community, according to statistics from the analytical company Alexa , is ahead of the social network MySpace in popularity . Sequoia Capital was the first major investor in YouTube ; from November 2005 to April 2006, it has invested in the project is 11.5 million dollars [18] [19] . According to some reports, another 25 million investment service managed to attract in April 2006 [20] . In March of the same year, the site began to display contextual advertising, however, according to experts, during the first years of its existence, the service did not bring any income to its owners. Another problem with YouTube is the large amount of content that violates the rights of rights holders to reproduce and copy material . Despite US law disclaiming responsibility for user-uploaded material and YouTube's assurance that all material posted in violation of the law was immediately removed from the site, several lawsuits were filed against the company by the fall of 2006. In particular, in September, representatives of the music label Universal Music announced their intention to sue on YouTube . At the time, many experts were predicting the fate of the popular Napster audio hosting site , which was shut down in 2000 due to claims by rights holders. [24] [25][13] . YouTube has made significant efforts to resolve copyright disputes. In September 2006,an agreement was signedbetween YouTube and Warner Music under which the servicewas allowed to publish material owned by Warner Musicin exchange for a portion of advertising revenue. In October of that year, similar agreements signed with Universal Music, as well as other major labels Sony BMG Music Entertainment and media company CBS [26] [27] . YouTube later signed cooperation agreements with other media giants, such as Time Warner Inc. and The Walt Disney Company [28] [29]. Now, in the case of substantiated claims, videos posted by users who do not have the copyright are removed from the site. In November 2006 , was completed purchase of YouTube by Google for 1.65 billion dollars [30] . The deal was Google Inc.'s biggest purchase. throughout the history of the company [31] [13] [21] . Before buying YouTube from Google, there was a similar service - Google Video . Google does not intend to close it, but will use it as a place to search for videos on all sites where you can post videos. Google Video Search now includes YouTube. Despite the fact that YouTube is the third most popular site in the world, it is not possible to make money on it, as Google CEO Eric Schmidt has repeatedly stated . Advertising does not bring the revenue that Google initially expected, and site maintenance is quite expensive. According to the analytical agency comScore, the cost of supporting YouTube in 2009 was $ 740 million; this is twice as much as the portal managed to earn. Google has long been trying to come up with new models that would allow the video service to make money. In 2008, the search engine agreed with Apple and Amazon to sell music through YouTube. In addition, Google is actively contracting withmedia holdings that post fragments of movies, TV series and TV shows on YouTube .
